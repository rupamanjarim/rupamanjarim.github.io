Research Interests

My research focuses on understanding the mechanisms underlying cardiac rhythm and arrhythmias through a combination of computational modeling, nonlinear dynamics, and experimental collaboration. I develop biophysically detailed models of cardiomyocytes and cardiac tissue to investigate how cellular electrophysiology, ion channel dynamics, and tissue structure interact to generate normal and pathological electrical activity in the heart. My work has contributed to the development of mathematical models of neonatal rat and adult pig atrial cardiomyocytes and to the study of spiral and scroll waves that drive complex arrhythmias. I am also interested in emerging approaches such as optogenetics for controlling and probing cardiac electrical activity. More recently, my research has expanded toward ion channel mechanisms and computational approaches for arrhythmia risk prediction. Through integrative modeling and interdisciplinary collaboration, my goal is to advance the mechanistic understanding of cardiac dynamics and contribute to improved strategies for diagnosis and therapy of cardiac rhythm disorders.
